das

I use the word das* to describe my aesthetic. It's a sort of matrix.

"Is this das?" ~~~ "That is not das" ~~~ "Oh this is SO DAS"

It's now the prefix I've given to a bunch of my projects. I love using it to be simultaneously pretencious (I am making Das Theatre—The Theatre!) but also not serious at all...

@ The Tank

The Tank is my main artistic home, because I resonate so deeply with the generosity in their support of performance artists.

You can catch me every first Tuesday participating in the Fast and Furious series.

As part of my life goal to transform the American Theatre, I took over producing the series Theater Sux at the Tank in 2019. Each themed edition, invites artists to create satirical scenes advocating for a less oppressive field.

dasParty (ongoing)

A literal pity party. Different stations are set up for people to visit in order to exorcise pity & fear. Disappointments from life are collected before the audience's arrival, and written on the party hats.

The stations include: "Pin the tail on the map where you've cried", "Spin the bottle, but instead of kissing someone, look into their eyes for 47 seconds", "Draw a photo of an ex you never want to see again (but blindfolded)", and the "Area to just be on your phone and not talk to anyone".

I staged this at the 2018 Edinburgh Fringe Festival one night, as a party for my fellow staff to destress and laugh in the middle of the madness.

Currently I'm creating a digital version of the live experience, you can play it here.

dasScript (ongoing)

I'm endlessly curious about documentation in a collaborative process, keeping track of source material/dramaturgy, and formatting.

It's an experiment with using websites as the collated document for performance-scripts, rather than Microsoft Word or Google Docs. Playscripts have looked the same for so long (character names in all caps, stage directions italicised, etc) and therefore shaped the imagination of artists.

dasChoreography (2019)

A dance piece collaging poses and gestures that feel connected to and representative of my queerness.

Workshoped in the Spring 2019 Fieldwork cohort, led by James Scruggs. Premiered in the August 2019 edition of MERDE at the Footlight Club featuring narration by Cosimo Pori.

dasArt (2018)

Subverting the form of gallery spaces (inspired by theories from Inside the White Cube: The Ideology of the Gallery Space by Brian O'Doherty), this piece blurs spectator/performer roles by handing each visitor a proxy through which they look at the artworks (ranging from photo to painting to video to performance art) and communicate with one another.

dasClassroom (2018)

The second das performance really saught to place "das" in the academic-aesthetic historical-discourse via a performance art lecture. I performed this 3 hour TED talk-esq experiment at Emerson College. It featured lip-syncing, reclamation of play-submission-feedback, sound collages, John Cage-ian boredom, a history of Das' origin, and more. Through this performance I began to realize that das is a reach towards the avant-garde independent European artists I love, but firmly planted in the history and path I have been on as an American.

Cabaret Das (2017)

The name and form is an homage to the Dada Cabaret Voltaire. Performed at the Huret and Spector Gallery on Emerson's campus in September 2017.

Nomi

Nomi is a theatrical work inspired by the life, aesthetic, and cultural impact of Klaus Sperber, aka Klaus Nomi. I constructed the script and worked with Riley Fox Hillyer to stage it for Theatre on Fire's Cabinet of Curiosities festival. It was performed at the Charlestown Working Theatre in May 2017.

*A brief history of das: During the fall of 2016, my colleagues at HowlRound were (jokingly) describing the performances they saw in Eastern Europe as "so das"... and I mistook it for some sort of academically cannonized word. It seemed to encapsulate everything I was drawn towards: anti-American/Aristotelian living room drama, via fluorescent lights and fake blood.
